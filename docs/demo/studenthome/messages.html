26 November 2016

Industrial Action Short of a Strike

On November 5th I wrote to tell you of the intention of the University and College Union (UCU) to begin industrial action involving a comprehensive boycott of setting and marking of assessments with effect from 6 November 2016.

I am now pleased to inform you that the UCU has suspended all industrial action from 20 November until after 15 January 2017. All assignments submitted from now on should be returned with a score/grade as well as comments. In the small number of cases where students have received feedback from their AL with comments but no score steps are being taken to ask the ALs involved to put a mark/grade on the assignments as soon as possible. If you are one of those students affected you will be notified when the score is available.

During the period of the action (6-19 November) a number of award meetings of Examination and Assessment Boards (EABs) did not take place as scheduled due to the industrial action taken by some of their members. We are now in the process of rescheduling these meetings to take place as soon as possible.

If you are one of those students whose results are likely to be delayed as a consequence we will write to you within the next week giving you an indication of when we expect the results on your module to become available.

I am delighted that the industrial action has been suspended and that for the majority of students it will have had no adverse impact.
